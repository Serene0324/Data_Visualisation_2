Population and Student Enrolments of Malaysia (2022)

This visualisation explores how Malaysia’s population is distributed across states and how student enrolment patterns compare to population size. Together, these charts reveal where education demand is concentrated and highlight demographic or infrastructural differences among states.

Population by State

States like Selangor, Johor, and Sabah dominate the population distribution, reflecting their larger urban areas and economic hubs. Meanwhile, smaller federal territories like Labuan and Putrajaya occupy a very small proportion of the total population.

Student Enrolments by State

Most states follow a balanced pattern, but Labuan and Putrajaya stand out as outliers — both have more student enrolments than residents.

This could indicate that many students from other states travel to study in Labuan and Putrajaya, where government schools and administrative facilities are highly concentrated. Their relatively small populations make even moderate enrolment numbers appear disproportionately large.

Student Enrolments by Education Stages and States

This stacked bar chart shows how student enrolments are distributed across primary, secondary, and post-secondary levels in each state.

Most states have the largest share in primary education, followed by secondary, and a much smaller share in post-secondary.

The higher number of primary students reflects Malaysia’s compulsory primary education (6 years) and broader age base at that level.

Fewer students in secondary and post-secondary levels may be due to dropouts, vocational training alternatives, or migration for higher education.

Gender Distribution of Student Enrolments

These diverging bars show the proportion of male (left) and female (right) students across all states, separated by education level (primary, secondary, post-secondary).

The gender distribution appears fairly balanced across most states, with minor female dominance in secondary and post-secondary levels.

This balance reflects Malaysia’s commitment to equal education access for both genders.

Slightly higher female participation in higher levels may suggest improving retention and performance rates among female students, a trend consistent with national education reports.

Together, these charts show how population, enrolment, and gender balance interact across Malaysia’s states — offering insights into demographic education patterns and regional disparities.

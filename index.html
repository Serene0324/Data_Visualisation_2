Population and Student Enrolments of Malaysia (2022)

This visualisation explores how Malaysia’s population is distributed across states and how student enrolment patterns compare to population size.
Together, these charts reveal where education demand is concentrated and highlight demographic or infrastructural differences among states.

Population by State

States like Selangor, Johor, and Sabah dominate the population distribution, reflecting their larger urban areas and economic hubs.
Meanwhile, smaller federal territories like Labuan and Putrajaya occupy a very small proportion of the total population.
This map provides geographic context for later charts, showing where most people live before comparing how many are actually enrolled in schools.

Total Population vs
Student Enrolments by State

Putrajaya exhibits a notably higher student ratio compared to other states.

This suggests that a significant number of students may come from outside the territory to study there, likely due to its concentration of government schools and administrative education institutions. The small local population further amplifies this ratio.

Student Enrolments by Education Stages and States

Most states have the highest share in primary education, followed by secondary and post-secondary. This reflects Malaysia’s compulsory six years of primary schooling and a broader age base at that level, while fewer students continue into higher levels due to dropouts, vocational paths, or migration for further studies.

Gender Distribution of
Student Enrolments

This diverging bar chart shows the overall gender distribution of student enrolments across all states, combining all education levels (primary, secondary and post-secondary).

Overall, Malaysia shows a fairly balanced number of male and female students across states, indicating equal access to education nationwide.
